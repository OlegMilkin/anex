Medical Thermal Imaging- Certification

NOT REQUIRED FOR FEVER SCREENING

Education is one of the most important aspects of medical thermography. There are certain standards that should be met in order for the thermographic information to be valid. A certified thermographic technician should receive training in patient procedures, patient protocols, room size, room temperature parameters, patient positioning, patient preparation, pre-scan requirements, acclimation period, patient data input and more!

Clinical certification training courses are available with independent board certified medical thermographers. For your convenience, clinical certification training courses are available on-line or in person. Spectron IR recommends the International Academy of Clinical Thermology (IACT) and the American Academy of Thermology (AAT) for your clinical certification training. Please call 855-482-6444 for more information on clinical certification courses, a copy of a clinical certification course outlines and clinical certification course availability.

Spectron IR provides full turn-key medical infrared solutions with complete online equipment training in the use of any of our imaging systems at NO extra charge. To see equipment packages click here.

Associations that teach medical thermal imaging must govern themselves and provide their own certification programs. That is why it is important for an aspiring certified medical thermography technician to ask questions about the course structure, length, and qualified trainers. The value of the certification is determined by the content and professional requirements. Spectron IR recommends that a person receives training from a board certified medical thermographer. A Spectron IR representative is available to answer your concerns via phone (855-482-6444) or email info@spectronir.com.

Example of Technician Course Syllabus:

Competency Areas

  1. Medical thermal imaging camera operations
  2. Imaging laboratory standards and protocols
  3. Patient preparation protocols and office procedures
  4. Routine projections of the head, thorax, and breast
  5. Routine projections of the upper extremities
  6. Routine projections of the lumbopelvic and abdominal region
  7. Routine projections of the lower extremities
  8. Special considerations: positioning and projections

Course Objectives:

1) Medical thermal imaging camera operations

  1. The student will be able to describe the necessary components of a medical thermal imaging camera.
  2. The student will be able to discuss the basic physics involved in medical infrared imaging as it pertains to the basic technological standards for medical infrared imaging systems.
  3. The student will be able to demonstrate competency in the operation of the medical imaging system:
    1. Patient data entry
    2. Data base use and functions
    3. Proper image quality settings (span, level, focus)
    4. Image capture
    5. Camera calibration (single point and complex)
    6. Physical manipulation of the imaging system components
    7. Exporting images

2) Imaging laboratory standards and protocols

  1. The student will be able to describe the proper physical needs of a functional thermal imaging laboratory.
  2. The student will be able to discuss the environmental demands and controls necessary for the capture of clinical infrared images.
  3. The student will be able to discuss the physiologic reasons for the laboratory environmental acclimation of the patient and its proper application.
  4. The student will be able to describe the proper maintenance of a thermal imaging laboratory.

3) Patient preparation protocols and office procedures

  1. The student will be able to list the pre-imaging preparation instructions given to each patient.
  2. The student will be able to discuss the physiological reasons for each item listed with regard to pre-imaging preparation of the patient.
  3. The student will be able to properly review patient intake forms for accuracy and lead the patient through the process.

4) Routine projections of the head, thorax, and breast

  1. The student will be able to discuss the routine and special projections/positions of the head, thorax, and breast in terms of structures visualized, general body considerations, and special body considerations.
  2. The student will be able to discuss the physiologic process of a thermoregulatory challenge and its proper application.
  3. The student will be able to evaluate the accuracy of positioning, image quality and anatomical structures visualized on thermographic images.
  4. The student will be able to apply knowledge of thermographic procedures related to the head, thorax, and breast via performance in a laboratory environment.
